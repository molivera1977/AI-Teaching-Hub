The Whale Shark

By Guy Belleranti

The whale shark is the biggest fish in the world. It is very big, but it is also very gentle. Its teeth are so small that it cannot bite or chew food.

A whale shark can be 25 feet long. Some grow to be 40 feet long. That is as long as a school bus!

How does such a big fish eat? The whale shark opens its big mouth. It sucks in water and tiny animals called plankton. Then it closes its mouth. The water goes out through its gills. But the food stays inside!

The gills have special parts called gill rakers. The gill rakers work like a net. They catch the tiny food so the shark can swallow it. The shark can also eat small fish and shrimp.

The whale shark’s gills also help it breathe. The gills take oxygen from the water. Then the heart sends the oxygen to the rest of the body.

Whale sharks have spots all over their bodies. Each whale shark has a different pattern of spots. Scientists look at the spots to tell one whale shark from another. The spots are like fingerprints!

Did you know? Whale sharks are so gentle that some people swim right next to them! Would you want to swim with a whale shark?
